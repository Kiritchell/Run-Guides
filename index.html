West Texas Runs

There have been many sites that you go on to search for possible runs especially when you're newer to running. In my experience most of these have inadequate information or are runs that I'm not looking for.

The goal for this site is to be a resource for new and seasoned runners to see what kind of runs are out there (specifically West Texas for now) and to get real information about the runs.

Long Runs

Canyon Lakes | 15 Miles
This is one of my favorite runs because of how much the scenery changes and it has very low traffic. Plus you run by multiple bodies of water and have a good bit of shade through parks!

RUNNER TIPS

Elevation change: 700'
Major road crossings 3 | University at Buddy Holly, Erskine at Canyon Lake 2, Broadway at Mackenzie Park, MLK at Dunbar.
Run Terrain The majority of the run can be done in the street on the park road. Between Aztlan Park and Buddy Holly Park will be a sidewalk or paved trail you can run on. The roads are minimally trafficed no matter time of day.
Tips If you park your car at the red parking icon in Mackenzie Park you will be in the middle of the two loops so you can run either direction first and then pit stop at your car before continuing.
Ransom Canyon | 14 Miles
Want a challenging run? This one puts both mind & body to the test because of the straight roads and then very steep hills out of the canyon.

RUNNER TIPS

Elevation change: 423'
Major road crossings 1 | CR 6900
Run Terrain The entirety of the run is on the road. When on the CR you will have at least a 6' shoulder between the lane and the dirt and the pavement was in good shape. Since it was relatively light on traffic I ran in the lane of opposing traffic for level ground.
Tips I parked at the very top of the hill so I could start on level ground. When you go down into the canyon you'll notice that you're on a stupid steep hill and yes, you'll have to run back up it so be ready. If you have to walk it doesn't mean you failed. I
